YouTube Search Experience Reimagined

YouTube has the best creator content in the world. But when you search to buy something or figure something out, it gives you a wall of videos and leaves the rest to you.

The two query types I focused on

1
Product discovery queries
"Best running shoes for beginners", "best laptop under $1000" , you know what you want to buy but YouTube gives you 40 review videos with no way to compare across them.
YouTube today ✦ Reimagined
Results Ranked list of videos Personalized to your runner type
Content 40 individual reviews 4-min synthesis stitched from 3 creators
Picks Every reviewer disagrees Gemini synthesizes across 18 reviews with specs and price
Navigation Scroll and hope Grouped by the decision you're making, with jump-points
2
Informational + exploratory
"How to break into AI PM", "how to start investing" , you want a clear path but there's a whole landscape to understand first. YouTube gives you 50 videos and no sense of where to begin.

Results Ranked list of videos Sequenced into a step-by-step action plan
Content 50 individual videos 4-min synthesis stitched from top career creators
Summary No overview Gemini Overview synthesizes what the transition actually requires
Navigation No structure Full topic landscape mapped so you explore what matters

How I approached this
Step 1
Found where YouTube search actually breaks

Navigational queries work fine. But product discovery and informational queries get the same flat ranked list — no structure, no starting point, no way to compare.

Step 2
The problem isn't the content. It's the interface.

YouTube has great creator content across every topic. What's missing is anything that helps users navigate it when the query is complex or decision-heavy.

Step 3
Gemini can do the hard work — if you surface it

Gemini already reads across YouTube's content at scale. The opportunity is to bring that understanding into the search UI without replacing the existing experience.

Step 4
Different intents need different layouts

Product discovery needs ranked picks and a decision framework. Informational needs a path and a topic map. Same system, different modules surfaced based on what Gemini detects.
